Latest Book Readings

Cloud Atlas

Cloud Atlas is a polyphonic compendium of interlacing but nonlinear parables. Divided into six different accounts spanning several centuries, Mitchell ranges from the journal of a 19th-century American notary to the post-apocalyptic memoir of a herdsman, Zachry. Each testament breaches time and space.

Lord of the Flies

Lord of the Flies, novel by William Golding, published in 1954. The book explores the dark side of human nature and stresses the importance of reason and intelligence as tools for dealing with the chaos of existence. In the novel, children are evacuated from Britain because of a nuclear war. One airplane, with adults and prep-school boys as passengers, crashes on an uninhabited island, and all the adults are killed. As the boys fashion their own society, their attempts at establishing a social order gradually devolve into savagery.

The Phantom of the Opera

The Phantom of the Opera tells the tale of a disfigured musical genius who haunts the Paris Opera House. Mesmerised by the talents and beauty of the young soprano Christine, the Phantom lures her as his protégé and falls fiercely in love with her. When’s Christine childhood sweetheart comes back into her life, the Phantom’s obsession sets the scene for a dramatic turn of events where jealousy, madness and passions collide.

The Good About Reading

For most people, reading is boring and hard to stay focused. Studies have show that reading have benefits, so you may read about them and think next time you are about to close a book. This is the list of reading benefits:

  1. Reduces Stress
  2. Promotes Wellness
  3. Helps Combat Alzheimer's and Dementia
  4. Helps with Sleep
  5. Improves Relationships
  6. Inspires Success
  7. Decreases Loneliness and Social Isolation

Old Books Are Not a Trash

Most of us have a lot of books in our house, and when years are passing through we may think to get rid of some. So this is a list of what to do with them:

  1. Donate to Thrift Stores and Secondhand Bookstores
  2. Find a Little Free Library
  3. Host a Book Swap
  4. Donate to Schools, Charities, or Libraries
  5. Leave Them on Your Stoop

How To Organize Your Books

Take a big breath and read the list below. Chose a way you like to organize your books.

  1. Separate your hardcovers and paperbacks
  2. Arrange your books by color
  3. Don’t be afraid to stack books
  4. Organize books by genre or subject
  5. Display your favorite books front and center
  6. Organize your books alphabetically
  7. Organize your books according to how they made you feel
  8. Group together the books you haven’t read yet
  9. Arrange your books by height and size
  10. Separate your books by author: contemporary or classic
  11. Organize books by the condition of their covers
  12. Separate fiction and nonfiction

Make a Book a Gift

A book is always a good gift for friends or family. But what book do i choose? Here is a list that can help you out:

  1. Think about what’s going on in their life
  2. Expand on known favourites
  3. Consider their aesthetic
  4. Commemorate something you’ve done together
  5. If all else fails, choose something you love
